Breaking News March 10, 2026

Jacob Heppner Reveals Hiking Injury in Hawaii — Unable to Submit 26.2 Score

Jacob Heppner revealed live on the CF Network that he fell while hiking in Hawaii and injured his wrist. The injury prevented him from completing and submitting a score for the 26.2 Open workout.

Heppner confirmed he will not compete in 26.3, meaning his 2026 Open is over. The former Games athlete has been a regular Open competitor and content creator in recent years, making this a notable absence from the leaderboard heading into the final week.

Source: CF Network YouTube

Top Story
March 10, 2026

26.2 Scores Are Final — Mertens and Campbell Lead Overall

The 26.2 leaderboard is locked. Colin Bosshard won the men's workout, Claudia Gluck won the women's. In the overall standings, Colten Mertens leads the men with 9 points. Jeffrey Adler sits second (15 pts), Thomas Stroier third (17 pts), and Jonne Koski and Peter Ellis are tied at fourth (18 pts each).

On the women's side, Lucy Campbell holds the overall lead at 6 points. Mirjam von Rohr is second (16 pts), Aline Wirz third (24 pts). The final 26.3 workout Thursday from Wodapalooza will determine who advances to Quarterfinals on March 26-30.

Source: CrossFit Games Leaderboard

March 10, 2026

Castro Week in Review: Portugal Trip, Sevan Head-to-Head, Games Volunteers

Dave Castro recapped his Portugal trip for the 26.2 announcement, did the workout head-to-head with Sevan (Sevan won), and announced the San Jose Games volunteer portal is open. He's heading to Miami this week for the 26.3 announcement at Wodapalooza.

Source: Dave Castro YouTube

March 10, 2026

Lucy Campbell Overtakes von Rohr — Leads the Women's Open

Lucy Campbell holds 6 overall points through two workouts, comfortably ahead of Mirjam von Rohr (16 pts). The 26.3 workout Thursday from Wodapalooza will determine who wins the 2026 Open.

Source: CrossFit Games Leaderboard
