The Fittest Coaches in CrossFit

Everyone always talks about the athletes, but we wanted to know who the fittest coaches are in the space.

Workout 26.1 (Sprint/Capacity Bias): Jordan Adcock – 1,114 | Tim Paulson – 941 | Angelo DiCicco – 5,209. Adcock and Paulson—both seasoned Games-level athletes—show the value of engine-first programming, something heavily emphasized in camps like PRVN Fitness and Mayhem Athlete.

Workout 26.2 (Skill / Efficiency Separator): Angelo DiCicco – 64 | Jordan Adcock – 387 | Tim Paulson – 2,477. This workout flipped the leaderboard. DiCicco’s dominant finish highlights the importance of movement efficiency and high volume gymnastics training.

Workout 26.3 (Grinder / High Volume): Angelo DiCicco – 212 | Jordan Adcock – 564 | Tim Paulson – 567. Again, DiCicco proves consistency across modalities—arguably the most “coach-proof” test of fitness.

Overall Standings: Jordan Adcock – 416 | Tim Paulson – 846 | Angelo DiCicco – 1,190. While DiCicco won two workouts, Adcock & Paulson’s consistency ultimately secured them the top overall ranks—mirroring how Games scoring rewards well-roundedness.

What This Says About the Coaching Landscape: CrossFit Mayhem remains the gold standard for volume and depth, consistently sending large contingents to the CrossFit Games with the PRVN camp steadily on their heels, having more top 10 athletes in the elite individual field and likely to surpass them this year in the number of overall athletes being sent to the CrossFit Games.

Non-Registered Athlete — Wajma Poosie: One coach who didn’t officially register for the Open still put up numbers worth talking about. Using the CrossFit Open’s rank-point system—where your overall score is the sum of your individual workout ranks (lower is better)—Poosie’s estimated results: 26.1 Rank ~793rd | 26.2 Rank 15th | 26.3 Rank ~2,126th | Estimated Total Points ~2,934 | Estimated Overall Rank ~620th worldwide. That places Poosie approximately 620th overall worldwide in the Men’s division out of 127,000+ competitors—putting him in the top 0.5% of all male Open athletes.

So… Who Are the Fittest Coaches? On the women’s side, Jordan Adcock is the fittest female coach in this field. Not because she won the most events—but because she never fell off. As a former Individual Semifinalist, four-time CrossFit Games team athlete, and current coach at PRVN Fitness, Adcock’s 2026 Open performance proves she doesn’t just develop elite athletes—she is one.

On the men’s side, Tim Paulson officially takes the top spot among registered coaches with an overall rank of 846. A 7x CrossFit Games athlete and PRVN coach, Paulson’s well-rounded performance across all three workouts earned him the title. However, it’s worth noting that Wajma Poosie—who didn’t officially register—would have ranked significantly higher at ~620th overall, making him arguably the fitter coach on paper. That’s the defining trait of elite coaching today: building athletes (and themselves) who can survive every test.

Breaking News March 18, 2026

Wodapalooza Aftermath: Weather Chaos, Travel Nightmares, and a Weekend That Tested Everyone

The 2026 Wodapalooza is in the books, and while the competition delivered some incredible moments, the aftermath has been anything but smooth for the hundreds of athletes, media, judges, volunteers, and families who traveled to Miami Beach.

Dense Updates recapped the entire weekend from start to finish. Thursday kicked off with the 26.3 live announcement at the venue, where Olivia Kerstetter, Danielle Brandon, and Arielle Loewen took on the workout. Arielle Loewen turned on the turbo in the final minute, passing Danielle Brandon by three reps. The elite individual competition followed, with James Sprague going three-for-three on event wins and Lucy Campbell taking the women’s overall title. The venue layout on Miami Beach was described as incredible, with the Gauntlet running throughout the weekend on Muscle Beach and Kill Taylor held at the spectator experience stage.

But the weather had other plans. Rain moved in during Thursday’s heats, and by the weekend, a massive storm system forced Wodapalooza to cancel the final day of outdoor competition. Elite Teams were relocated to CrossFit Miami Beach to complete 26.3 in a closed-door setting with no spectators — only broadcast crew and judges were allowed inside. Local CrossFit affiliates in Miami rallied to open their doors so community division athletes could still complete the Open workout before the submission deadline, since many CrossFit gyms were closed on Sunday.

The real nightmare began Monday. A massive weather system collided with spring break travel and a partial government shutdown that left TSA short-staffed. Miami International Airport saw 270 delays and 267 cancellations by mid-afternoon Monday alone, according to FlightAware. Over 1,800 flights were cancelled nationally. Athletes like Jayson Hopper and Justin Medeiros, along with competitors like Quinn Robinson, found themselves stuck at the airport with no way home. Media members including Bella Martin and Chase Ingraham were in the same situation. Families who had traveled with athletes, judges, and volunteers who gave their time to make the event happen were all caught in the chaos — facing rebooking nightmares, sold-out rental cars, and hours-long lines at the terminal. Some ended up renting Sprinter vans or extending Airbnb stays just to wait it out. Dense Updates himself dealt with delay after delay before finally boarding a flight late Monday night.

Despite the disruptions, Dense Updates noted that the overall competition was a success. The elite individual prize purse was as follows: 1st — $75,000, 2nd — $30,000, 3rd — $20,000, 4th — $10,000, 5th — $6,000, 6th — $5,000, 7th — $4,000, 8th — $3,000, 9th — $2,000, 10th — $1,000. The community divisions brought in over a thousand athletes, and the venue itself was praised by competitors and fans alike. The city of Miami provided Wodapalooza $800,000 to host the event. But the combination of weather cancellations, a lack of communication at times, and the Monday travel disaster left a bittersweet taste for many who made the trip.

Source: Dense Updates YouTube, Local 10, CBS Miami, FlightAware

Colten Mertens & Lucy Campbell Win the 2026 CrossFit Open (Unofficial)

Colten Mertens (USA) and Lucy Campbell (GBR) have unofficially claimed the top overall spots in the 2026 CrossFit Open. Mertens dominated with 9 total points across three workouts, while Campbell accumulated 17 points to edge out a competitive women’s field. Mertens won the final workout outright, and his consistency across all three weeks proved unbeatable.

26.1

Mertens finished 3rd on 26.1 with a time of 11:16, behind the event winners. Peter Ellis (AUS) posted 11:33 for 10th. Jeffrey Adler (CAN) took 9th at 11:30, while Jonne Koski (FIN) placed 15th and Nika Maisuradze (GEO) came in 14th. On the women’s side, Mirjam Von Rohr (CHE) won the event with a blazing 9:50, while Campbell placed 2nd at 10:34. Leah Støren (NOR) was 14th, Emma Lawson (CAN) landed 29th, and Morgane Thyssens (BEL) finished 21st.

26.2

Mertens placed 5th on 26.2 with a time of 6:20, tied with Adler. Koski surged to 2nd with 6:10, while Ellis took 7th at 6:22 and Maisuradze was 15th at 6:33. Campbell finished 4th with 6:50. Von Rohr slipped to 15th at 7:15, Støren was 22nd, Lawson 13th, and Thyssens 29th. The middle week reshuffled the overall standings significantly.

26.3

Mertens sealed the overall title by winning 26.3 outright with a time of 13:46. Ellis finished 3rd at 15:10, while Adler placed 13th with 287 reps and Koski dropped to 20th with 283 reps. Maisuradze held 8th at 15:51. Campbell posted 14:29 for 11th on the final workout, but her consistency over the first two weeks kept her safely ahead. Von Rohr climbed back to 3rd overall with a 13:35 finish (3rd on 26.3). Støren placed 13th, Lawson 10th, and Thyssens 15th.

WZA Elite Teams Finish 26.3 at CrossFit Miami Beach — Just Girls Take the Lead

After Wodapalooza officially cancelled the final day of outdoor competition due to severe weather, remaining Elite Teams were relocated to CrossFit Miami Beach to complete Open workout 26.3. Only special media personnel and judges were allowed inside the venue.

Breaking News March 15, 2026

WZA Officially Cancels Remaining Competition Due to Severe Weather

Wodapalooza has officially announced that all remaining competition for the final day has been cancelled due to ongoing severe weather in the Miami Beach area. Results are now final for all community divisions. The decision was made with the safety of athletes, staff, volunteers, and fans as the top priority.

Only one heat of men completed CrossFit Open workout 26.3 this morning before lightning forced a shelter-in-place order and a series of delays that pushed competition from 1:30 PM to 2:30 PM to 4:00 PM. With two heats of 26.3 plus Sunburn and All Hands On Deck still on the schedule, there was simply no window left to resume.

At the time of cancellation, Team GoWod (Justin Medeiros, Dallin Pepper, Jayson Hopper) led the men’s field with 280 points after three events, and Yeti Girls (Danielle Brandon, Emily Rolfe, Alex Gazan) led the women’s side with 270 points. Elite Teams were later relocated to CrossFit Miami Beach to complete 26.3, which changed the final standings.

WZA also acknowledged that many athletes and spectators still need to complete Open workout 26.3. Several local gyms have opened their doors and waived drop-in fees for Sunday evening: CrossFit Wynwood (until 9 PM), Wynwood Training Club (until 9 PM), Peak360 CrossFit (until 8 PM), and Caution CrossFit in Miami Lakes (until 9 PM).

This is the second cancellation of Elite events at this year’s Gymreapers Wodapalooza after the men’s individual final was also scrapped due to lightning on Day 2. For questions, WZA directed the community to reach out at team@wzasports.com.

Source: Wodapalooza Official Instagram (@wodapalooza)

Breaking News March 15, 2026

Will WZA Cancel the Final Day? Repeated Delays Leave Elite Teams in Limbo

The final day of Gymreapers Wodapalooza Elite Team competition is on the verge of falling apart. Only one heat of men completed CrossFit Open workout 26.3 this morning before lightning was detected within 20 miles, forcing an immediate stoppage and a shelter-in-place order for everyone at the venue.

Since then, the delays have stacked up. Competition was first pushed to 1:30 PM, then to 2:30 PM, and now Wodapalooza has announced a further delay until 4:00 PM. With two remaining heats of 26.3 plus two full events — Sunburn and All Hands On Deck — still on the schedule, organizers are running out of daylight and options.

This is the second major weather disruption of the week. On Day 2, lightning forced the cancellation of remaining Elite Individual events, making those results final. James Sprague and Kensie Campbell were crowned WZA champions after only three of five scheduled events were completed. Now the Elite Teams division faces the same scenario.

The timing could not be worse for WZA Sports, which is operating under new ownership this year with Gymreapers stepping in as the title sponsor. This is also the first time Wodapalooza has been held in March rather than its traditional January window. While the later date was intended to avoid schedule conflicts with the CrossFit Open season, it has put the event squarely in South Florida’s early storm season. Weather is an act of god — no one can control it — but the pattern of repeated lightning delays across multiple competition days raises questions about whether the March calendar slot is sustainable for an outdoor event in Miami Beach.

The forecast is not encouraging. Scattered thunderstorms are expected to return by late afternoon, leaving a narrow window — if any — to resume competition. If conditions don’t improve, organizers may have no choice but to cancel the remaining events and finalize standings based on the three events already completed.

Four Women Within 6 Points Heading Into WZA Finale — Moistus, Domit, Campbell & Loewen All in the Hunt

The women’s title race at Gymreapers Wodapalooza Miami Beach is going down to the wire. After four events, Andra Moistus and Abigail Domit are tied at 258 points each atop the leaderboard, with Lucy Campbell (254) and Arielle Loewen (252) right behind them. Just 6 points separate first from fourth heading into the final event of the weekend — Press & Impress.

Moistus has been the most dominant athlete on the floor with two event wins (Event 1 and Complex, Actually), but a 16th-place finish on Event 4 (Heavy After Dark) opened the door. Domit has been the steadiest of the group — no event wins, but a 2nd, 3rd, and 9th have kept her locked in at the top. Campbell held the overall lead after Day 1 but slipped to third after a 13th-place finish on the barbell event. Loewen won Event 2 (No Easy Way Down) and sits just 2 points back of Campbell in fourth.

Astrid Tind rounds out the top 5 at 210 points but would need a massive performance and a collapse from the leaders to make a move.

The final event — Press & Impress — is 3 rounds for time of 15 dumbbell bench press, 50-ft front-rack dumbbell lunge, and 100-ft handstand walk (70/50 lb). The handstand walk component could be the great equalizer — or the deciding factor.
