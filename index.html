Jacob Heppner Reveals Hiking Injury in Hawaii — Unable to Submit 26.2 Score

Jacob Heppner revealed live on the CF Network that he fell while hiking in Hawaii and injured his wrist. The injury prevented him from completing and submitting a score for the 26.2 Open workout.

Heppner confirmed he will not compete in 26.3, meaning his 2026 Open is over. The former Games athlete has been a regular Open competitor and content creator in recent years, making this a notable absence from the leaderboard heading into the final week.

Source: CF Network YouTube

March 10, 2026

26.2 Scores Are Final — Mertens and Campbell Lead Overall

The 26.2 leaderboard is locked. Colin Bosshard won the men's workout, Claudia Gluck won the women's. In the overall standings, Colten Mertens leads the men with 9 points. Jeffrey Adler sits second (15 pts), Thomas Stroier third (17 pts), and Jonne Koski and Peter Ellis are tied at fourth (18 pts each).

On the women's side, Lucy Campbell holds the overall lead at 6 points. Mirjam von Rohr is second (16 pts), Aline Wirz third (24 pts). The final 26.3 workout Thursday from Wodapalooza will determine who advances to Quarterfinals on March 26-30.

Source: CrossFit Games Leaderboard

Making Amends

Hiller Apologizes to Taylor Self — Live on CF Network

Andrew Hiller joined Sevan and Taylor Self live on the CF Network YouTube channel for a roughly 30-minute conversation. Hiller opened by apologizing to Taylor. He said it "rips at his brain" having friends in the space while also wanting to make content, and that he felt like a hypocrite for not making certain videos because of his loyalty to the group. He originally planned to wait until Wodapalooza to talk in person but decided not to let it go on any longer. Sevan said the trust in the original group was "crazy" and that when three people (JY, Tyler, and Hiller) left, it "rocked" them. Taylor said he doesn't hate Hiller and the conversation appeared genuine.

🤔
Still Unresolved

What About Tyler Watkins?

During the Hiller apology conversation, Taylor brought up Tyler Watkins specifically. Taylor called out what he described as "objective lies" — Tyler telling people they kicked him out of the group, that he hates phone calls, and that he was friends with Brian Friendly. Hiller acknowledged the group fallout but didn't directly address Tyler's side. With Hiller and Taylor clearing the air, the question remains whether Tyler will also address the situation.

Competition

Taylor Self Wins Fittest Coach in Charlotte — Opponent Caught Cheating

Taylor Self won the Fittest Coach competition in Charlotte, live-streamed on the CF Network YouTube channel, taking home $1,000. During the event, Taylor confronted an opponent who allegedly started the assault bike with 4-5 calories already on the screen. Dense Updates noted that some competitors were "butt hurt" that the fittest coach in Charlotte actually came to win it.

💥
Face Off

Dave Castro Shows Up at Sevan's House — They Do 26.2 Together

Dave Castro made a surprise appearance at Sevan Matossian's Games Update Show, just showing up at his house. The next morning, the two did 26.2 together live at CrossFit West Santa Cruz. Dave made it to the muscle-ups but couldn't get any. Sevan made it through the chest-to-bar and back to the dumbbell. Dense Updates noted people enjoy seeing media personalities put themselves out there and open to public scrutiny in the live chats.

Breaking

CEO Don Faul Quits Mid-Open — 3 Days Notice

Don Faul resigned as CrossFit CEO with approximately 3 days notice during the Open. His last day was March 6th. Dense Updates covered the resignation, noting it came right as the Fittest documentary launched on Apple TV and Amazon Prime on March 3rd. The team meeting about his departure happened the same day as the documentary release during the second week of the Open.

Documentary

The Fittest Documentary — Tia "Oozes Authenticity"

The 2025 CrossFit Games documentary is now streaming on Apple TV and Amazon Prime. Dense Updates described Tia-Clair Toomey as someone who "oozes authenticity" in the film. The documentary covers the controversial pegboard event with a conversation between Dave Castro and Tia after the event. Dense Updates noted how competitive Tia is and how frustrated she gets when events don't go her way.
