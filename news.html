Semifinals April 2, 2026

Games Tickets on the Line at Mayhem Classic

The Mayhem Classic is just two weeks away and the field is stacked with athletes who have already made their mark this season. Jeffery Adler and Lucy Campbell have finished the highest across the two tests so far. Adler has taken 3rd in both the Open and Quarterfinals while Campbell won the Open and then finished 4th in the Quarterfinals. With both of them heading to Cookeville the question now is whether or not this is a preview of them standing on top of the podium at the Mayhem Classic.

Mayhem will send three men and three women to this years CrossFit Games and there is no shortage of contenders for those spots. Jayson Hopper the reigning Fittest on Earth has quietly gone 26th in the Open and 20th in the Quarterfinals which keeps him firmly in the mix even without a headline grabbing finish yet. Austin Hatfield the hometown favorite who trains at Mayhem placed 2nd in Quarterfinals after taking 50th in the Open and he already proved he can win on this floor by winning last years Mayhem Classic and punching his ticket to the Games. Fan favorite Colten Mertens the "Iron Hog" who won this years Open, finished 7th in Quarterfinals and also earned his Games spot last season alongside Hatfield. Colten can not be ruled out for one of those three qualifying places.

On the women's side Abigail Domit also secured her ticket to the Games at Mayhem Classic last year and has already shown she thrives under this style of programming. Gabi Migala is returning to the individual semifinals floor for the first time since her injury at 2024 Rogue Invitational where she was sitting in 2nd overall behind Tia-Clair Toomey-Orr after five events before suffering a torn achilles during the Duel. Fans are eager to see what Gabi will do this year and if she can make it back to the CrossFit Games. Another serious threat for one of the three spots is Olivia Kerstetter who stood on the podium in 3rd at the 2026 CrossFit Games.

Without knowing the workouts yet, it is difficult to predict exactly how the leaderboard will shake out. What we do know is that Mayhem Classic has a distinctive programming style and history tells us to expect classic Mayhem elements such as GHD sit-ups, toes to bar, handstand walking, rope climbs and running, which is already rumored to be a trail run this year. Angelo DiCicco mentioned on the CrossFit Games podcast with host Chase Ingraham that the Mayhem media team will again release a workout teaser graphic similar to last year and that they are partnering with PBS to broadcast live coverage of the Saturday and Sunday action for fans who cannot make the trip to Cookeville.

When the last heat clears the floor, three men and three women will be holding Games tickets and everyone watching will have witnessed a pivotal chapter in this season story.

Semifinals March 31, 2026

Gymreapers Steps In as Mayhem Classic Sponsor

Gymreapers has been announced as the official performance partner of the upcoming Mayhem Classic. The move continues Gymreapers’ rapid expansion in the space first for Miami’s Wodapalooza and now Mayhem Classic, signaling the brand’s intent to put it's stamp firmly on the CrossFit space.

In a series of new social posts, Mayhem Classic revealed Gymreapers as its presenting sponsor, and confirmed a total prize purse payout of $59,000 dollars, to be distributed across the top 5 men and top 5 women, with first place on each side taking home $12,000 dollars. On top of that, these earnings will sit alongside any CrossFit Games Semifinals payouts, as the 2026 CrossFit Games rulebook states that cash prizes will be awarded to the top individual athletes at Semifinals.

The Mayhem Classic runs April 17th to 19th, and with just a couple of weeks to go, tickets are still on sale through Ticket Tailor via the link in the Mayhem Classic Instagram bio or on their website which you can find here on our Season Info page.

Quarterfinals March 30, 2026

Consistency Wins: Crouch and Milligan Unofficially Win Quarterfinals

Jay Crouch and Kyra Milligan, both PRVN athletes, are unofficially your winners of the individual Quarterfinals, winning on consistency across all four tests. Crouch and Milligan’s wins come from stacking solid, top-tier finishes in each event. Jay logged a 3rd, 11th, 2nd, and 19th place finish being his worst result. Kyra took a win on Event 3, the deadlift and double-under workout, plus a 3rd and an 8th, with her worst finish being 24th.

Multiple individual event winners are currently buried well down the overall leaderboard, which suggests there are some incorrect times entered. There are athletes who took first or podium in a single test, but the rest of their scores are noticeably lower on the leaderboard. For example, Chantelle Graham of SoLa CrossFit is showing a 1st place finish on Event 1 with a time of 4:42, while her remaining scores range from 4,146th place to as low as 7,673rd. Another example is Jacob Klatkiewicz of Driftless CrossFit, with a 1st place finish on Event 2 in 5:20, which is a minute faster than Ricky Garard in 2nd, yet Jacob’s other scores range from 1,614th place to 7,033rd.

Several long running issues with the online leaderboard are showing up again this year, and many of them come down to how scores are entered and displayed. We are certain to see some waves of real time score changes after CrossFit reviews this leaderboard, which will likely reshuffle the standings slightly.

According to the CrossFit Games rulebook, section 2.03, if a score is not validated by the affiliate manager by Wednesday at 5 p.m. PT, the score will no longer be visible on the leaderboard. The rulebook also states that CrossFit will request videos from top performances to review and that review process will be used primarily for prize money purposes. The Individual and Age Group leaderboards will be set no later than April 10, 2026.

Quarterfinals March 30, 2026

Mertens Crushes Quarterfinals, Wins Three of Four Live Streamed Events

Fans tuning into this weekend’s CrossFit Games Quarterfinals live streams were treated to a breakout performance from Colten Mertens, who topped the unofficial leaderboard in three of the four workouts.

Using times gathered from athletes who chose to live stream their attempts on YouTube, Mertens sits first in Workouts 1, 2, and 4, and only misses a clean sweep by 22 seconds in Workout 3. While the official worldwide leaderboard will ultimately tell the full story, this sample of Games and Semifinal athletes highlights just how impressive his weekend really was.

Mertens set the tone early in Workout 2 with a time of 7:16, and backed it up again in Workout 1 with a time of 5:44 and tightening his grip on the competition with a top time on Workout 4 of 9:28. The only blemish on an otherwise perfect run came in Workout 3. Tudor Magda stole the show there with a blazing 3:59, while Mertens’ finished at 4:21 leaving him just outside the top spots among the streamed performances. Still, the consistency across all four tests once again proved that he is hanging with some of the fittest names in the sport.

As fans wait for the official CrossFit Games Quarterfinals standings to finalize, these live streamed performances offer an exciting preview of what might be coming later this season. If this weekend is any indication, Colten Mertens is not just ready to qualify for the CrossFit Games again, he is ready to contend.

Top Story March 27, 2026

3 Peak Challenge in Honor of McCoy “Topsy” Turner

Next weekend, three familiar names in the CrossFit community Rob Lawson, Dafydd Dennis, and Dave Parfitt will take on one of the most physically demanding tribute challenges you’ll see this year. This isn’t about fitness for the sake of fitness, but about honoring a close friend, continuing his mission, and supporting the family he left behind.

The trio will attempt the British Three Peaks Challenge. Starting at Ben Nevis in Scotland, then moving to Scafell Pike in England, and finishing at Snowdon in Wales, they’ll hike each mountain carrying a rucksack and row 10,000 meters at every summit before descending and driving to the next peak.

This challenge mirrors an incredible feat completed by their close friend, McCoy “Topsy” Turner, who climbed Mount Batur in Bali carrying a rowing machine, rowed 10,000 meters at the top, and descended to raise awareness and funds for PTSD and mental health.

McCoy, a former Royal Navy Physical Training Instructor and CrossFit Level 4 coach, spent 17 years in the military, including a deployment to Afghanistan, before continuing his mission of service through CrossFit. He delivered more than 250 Level 1 and Level 2 seminars across Europe and Asia, mentoring coaches and building communities everywhere he went.

Behind his strength and humor, McCoy was fighting a long, often silent battle with complex PTSD, and on March 8, 2025, he died by suicide. In the wake of that loss, his friends are determined to carry his message forward, using this challenge to raise awareness for men’s mental health and support those who are still struggling in silence.

The fundraising effort is focused on McCoy’s two sons, Brodie and Tommy, helping provide support as they navigate life without their dad. Since losing his father, Brodie has earned a place at university to study medicine, a powerful reflection of the resilience and values McCoy instilled.

Friends, military oppo’s, and the global CrossFit community now have an opportunity to rally once more. This time to support McCoy’s boys and continue his legacy of strength, service, and care for others.

The Barbell Spin Takes Shots at CF Network News, Sevan Matossian, and Dense Updates

The Barbell Spin posted to Instagram yesterday going after Sevan Matossian, Jenny from Dense Updates, and this website. The caption read, “@sevanmatossian thinks AI slop is the fastest, most reliable news source in the space. It’s no wonder @dense_updates acts like she isn’t behind @cfnetworknews. I wouldn’t want to be either.”

The post included a screenshot of our article “The Cheater Finally Admits It” alongside an AI detection tool, attempting to discredit the reporting as machine generated content. The post pulled 411 likes and 127 comments.

It is pretty clear from the caption that his feelings are hurt. Sevan deemed another news source superior and it appears that didn’t sit well with Spin. Rather than competing with better coverage, The Barbell Spin chose to attack. The post reads less like media commentary and more like a public meltdown from someone who feels threatened by a fresh, innovative site that offers top stories, daily podcast and show schedules, channel rankings, gossip, and more, all in one place.

Over the years there have been grammatical errors, spelling errors, redundant wording, and incorrect quotation marks on The Barbell Spin’s website, but no one has ever called him out on it. You will get those kinds of things from time to time with human error as nobody is perfect. Unless of course Spin thinks he is?

For context, The Barbell Spin has been around for many years in the CrossFit media space. In that time, the website has barely evolved. It still looks like something built in the early days of the internet. Meanwhile, we found four grammar and spelling errors across just three of their most recent articles, including a sentence that literally reads, “The second stage of the 2026 CrossFit Games season is upon.” Upon what? Upon who? We will let the readers decide who is producing slop.
