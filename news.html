Kerstetter Wins 26.3 Live at Miami — Moistus Dominates WZA Elite, No Men Finish

Olivia Kerstetter took the win in the CrossFit Open 26.3 live announcement at Gymreapers Wodapalooza Miami Beach on Thursday afternoon. The workout — a grueling for-time chipper of burpees over the bar, cleans, and thrusters at ascending weights with a 16-minute time cap — tested every athlete on the beach.

Danielle Brandon was in position to finish second but Arielle Loewen surged in the closing minutes and edged Brandon out by a few reps to claim the runner-up spot. Kerstetter, the 20-year-old who finished third at the 2025 Games, continues to prove she belongs at the top of the sport.

When the WZA elite individual women took the floor for Event 1 — which was 26.3 itself — Estonia’s Andra Moistus won with a finishing time of 14:16. Only four other women completed the workout under the 16-minute cap. On the men’s side, not a single elite athlete finished. James Sprague came the closest, falling just one rep short of completing the workout. The result underscores just how punishing 26.3 is at the prescribed elite weights.

Open scores for 26.3 are due by Monday, March 16 at 5 PM PT. The WZA elite competition continues through Sunday.

Source: CrossFit Games YouTube — 26.3 LIVE Announcement | CrossFit Games

Wodapalooza Expands to Mexico City — Three-Day Event Set for December 2026

Wodapalooza announced it will expand to Mexico City with a three-day event scheduled for December 4–6, 2026, marking the brand’s third location alongside Miami Beach and Southern California. The 2024 launch of WZA SoCal proved the model could scale beyond South Florida. Mexico City represents the next step — taking the brand international. Registration details expected later this year.

Two elite men are out of Wodapalooza Miami Beach on Day 1. Bill Leahy withdrew due to a back injury and Connor Voltz pulled out with an ankle injury. PushPress CEO Calls Out Berkshire Partners in Open Letter — “They Are Killing CrossFit”

PushPress CEO Dan Uyemura published an open letter to Berkshire Partners accusing the private equity firm of destroying CrossFit from the inside. Uyemura argues CrossFit’s entire value is its community and affiliate network. He points to failed buyer negotiations, 2025 Open registrations dropping 32%, and Hyrox growing to 650,000 competitors. He demands Berkshire hire a community-first CEO with real authority.

Mayhem Classic Will Be Livestreamed Free — Froning & McKernan Confirm Full Broadcast

Rich Froning and Rory McKernan confirmed on The Rich Froning Podcast that the 2026 Mayhem Classic will be fully livestreamed and free to watch.
