Community April 6, 2026

The Relish Project

The new film on YouTube by Justin Medeiros, “The Relish Project”, is a celebration of CrossFit’s everyday community, not a polished Games hype video. A 10 year reunion road trip, the project brings Justin Medeiros along side his videographer Dallas Hamm and longtime friends Sun, Bob, and Zach back together, to drive from Lodi, CA down to Huntington Beach, CA, dropping into boxes, running partner workouts, and letting the story unfold gym by gym instead of following a rigid plan.

What makes the video stand out is what it chooses to celebrate. The crew highlights members in local affiliates. Micah, a young athlete back in the gym just 12 weeks after open heart surgery, an 87 year old grandmother who has been doing CrossFit since 2008, a dad who calls the gym his “home away from home,” and moms like Denise who leaned on their affiliates to navigate postpartum recovery and loneliness. At the Ranch, at gritty old school spots like Seabright CrossFit, and at long running affiliates like CrossFit Inferno, home to Bill Grundler, Games-level athletes sweat next to everyday members, sharing hill sprints, wall balls, deadlifts, and stories in the same class.

For a sport that often spotlights podiums, the Relish Project feels refreshing because Medeiros and his friends deliberately chase connection by camping in a van, getting lost, laughing through brutal WODs, and sometimes turning the cameras off just to hang out. Seeing a two time CrossFit Games champion choose to invest his time and his platform into an affiliate road trip, rather than only Games prep content, sends a clear message: the heart of CrossFit is still the local box.

Top Story March 27, 2026

3 Peak Challenge in Honor of McCoy “Topsy” Turner

Next weekend, three familiar names in the CrossFit community Rob Lawson, Dafydd Dennis, and Dave Parfitt will take on one of the most physically demanding tribute challenges you’ll see this year. This isn’t about fitness for the sake of fitness, but about honoring a close friend, continuing his mission, and supporting the family he left behind.

The trio will attempt the British Three Peaks Challenge. Starting at Ben Nevis in Scotland, then moving to Scafell Pike in England, and finishing at Snowdon in Wales, they’ll hike each mountain carrying a rucksack and row 10,000 meters at every summit before descending and driving to the next peak.

This challenge mirrors an incredible feat completed by their close friend, McCoy “Topsy” Turner, who climbed Mount Batur in Bali carrying a rowing machine, rowed 10,000 meters at the top, and descended to raise awareness and funds for PTSD and mental health.

McCoy, a former Royal Navy Physical Training Instructor and CrossFit Level 4 coach, spent 17 years in the military, including a deployment to Afghanistan, before continuing his mission of service through CrossFit. He delivered more than 250 Level 1 and Level 2 seminars across Europe and Asia, mentoring coaches and building communities everywhere he went.

Behind his strength and humor, McCoy was fighting a long, often silent battle with complex PTSD, and on March 8, 2025, he died by suicide. In the wake of that loss, his friends are determined to carry his message forward, using this challenge to raise awareness for men’s mental health and support those who are still struggling in silence.

The fundraising effort is focused on McCoy’s two sons, Brodie and Tommy, helping provide support as they navigate life without their dad. Since losing his father, Brodie has earned a place at university to study medicine, a powerful reflection of the resilience and values McCoy instilled.

Friends, military oppo’s, and the global CrossFit community now have an opportunity to rally once more. This time to support McCoy’s boys and continue his legacy of strength, service, and care for others.
