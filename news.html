Competition April 9, 2026

Mayhem Releases Event 3: The Dirty 30s

“The Dirty 30s” has been announced as Event 3 of the Mayhem Classic. This event will likely be the second workout on Day Two. The workout features 30/24 rep segments of ring & bar muscle ups, sandwhiched in between segments of sandbag squats, with 150 pounds for the men and 125 pounds for the women.

The exact weekend schedule is still yet to be confirmed, including whether Event 4 will close out Saturday or begin the final day, and whether the format will mirror last years with two workouts on Saturday and three on Sunday. With muscle ups & sandbag squats that are typically done with the bag racked on athletes back, that still leaves 30 workouts in play for the events still to be announced. 90 squats is not "too much" for these elites athletes so we are not ruling out thrusters or front squats to still show up in the programming. It also remains unknown how many total workouts will ultimately be programmed for the competition.

Leaderboard Shake Up April 8, 2026

Jay Crouch Gets Major Penalty

Jay Crouch has received a one minute penalty on Event 1 of the CrossFit Quarterfinals, moving his score from 2nd on the workout all the way to 284th on that test. That single hit drops him to 38th overall on the mens leaderboard.

There is no word yet on whether more penalties are coming for the men, but CrossFit has said the leaderboard will be finalized no later than April 10, leaving athletes and fans refreshing the standings for the next two days. So far, there are no comparable major penalties showing up on the womens side.

Community April 7, 2026

CrossFit Community Mourns the loss of Legend Rob Orlando

Rob Orlando, one of CrossFit’s original legends, the “strongman of CrossFit,” has died after his battle with cancer. His passing was shared in a tribute post on his Instagram account that read, “April 28, 1975 - April 6, 2026. Yours in strength - Rob O.”

Orlando was a four time CrossFit Games athlete and the founder of Hybrid Athletics, he helped bring strongman movements like carries and odd object work into mainstream CrossFit programming. For many in the sport, he represented the toughness and personality of CrossFit’s early era.

Rob was first diagnosed in January of 2025, and underwent surgery to remove part of his jaw, and then went through radiation and chemotherapy, losing nearly 30 pounds in the process, weight he was determined to earn back.

In the comments and across the community, athletes, coaches, and fans remembered Orlando as a generous coach, a larger than life presence, and a major influence on the way strength is trained in affiliates today. The overwhelming message was simple, CrossFit lost one of it's foundational figures.

Source: Instagram post on Rob Orlando’s account @crossfitrobo
