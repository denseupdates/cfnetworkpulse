Breaking Gossip

Chief Peoples Officer Out?

Andrew Hiller posted a photo of Annette Reavis, CrossFit’s Chief Peoples Officer, to his Instagram account with the single-word caption “Hope.” The post, which was liked by Matthew Souza among others, has generated significant speculation about Reavis’s future with the company.

After Don Faul announced his immediate departure on March 3rd — telling his team his final day would be Friday, March 6th — the CF Network was live with Sevan Matossian, Matthew Souza, Peter White, and Jenny Hull to discuss the CEO stepping down. During the show, they questioned whether Annette Reavis, who has been widely regarded as Don Faul’s right hand at CrossFit, would remain on staff. Hiller’s post all but suggests this is the case. Adding fuel to the speculation, Daniel Chaffey commented on Hiller’s post: “Today the team of CrossFit is the best it has been for years.”

Reports are coming in that she has been removed from Slack, the program the company uses for internal communications amongst their team members. We will update you with more information as it becomes available.

Breaking Gossip

Hiller Leaves WZA After Access Dispute — “I’m Packing Up My Shit”

According to Sevan Matossian on the CF Network YouTube channel, Andrew Hiller was allegedly paid by Wodapalooza to come to Miami and cover the event. The deal allegedly included hotel, airfare, and smart glasses, and was arranged through Dylan. Hiller had one stipulation before accepting: complete floor access. No one could tell him where he could or couldn’t go, and he was told that would be honored — a level of access no one else received.

The issue arose because two events were running simultaneously at the venue — Wodapalooza and the CrossFit Open 26.3 live announcement, which was operated by CrossFit. When Hiller went into the warm-up area during the CrossFit-run portion, he was told he couldn’t be back there. Hiller allegedly pushed back, citing his full-access deal. The response: his access covered Wodapalooza only, not the CrossFit event.

Hiller had also warned ahead of time that if he had any issues, he was out. After the back-and-forth, he allegedly filmed the entire exchange with his smart glasses, allegedly said “fine, cool, no problem — I’m packing up my shit and going home,” and left Miami.

Hot Take

Hiller Gives the 2026 Open a C-Minus — “The Worst CrossFit Open of All Time”

Andrew Hiller posted a 14-minute breakdown of the 2026 CrossFit Open, grading it a C-minus overall. His main complaints: all three workouts were medium-length time caps disguised as AMRAPs, there were no repeats, no heavy strength tests (the heaviest barbell was 135 lb for men), and too many thrusters and squats across the three weeks. He said the Open cannot identify the 100 fittest athletes without quarterfinals doing the heavy lifting. Hiller noted women had an advantage on 26.3 since the final bar was only 85 lb compared to 135 lb for men. He also flagged scoring issues from 26.2 where a single muscle-up could swing rankings by 18,000 to 50,000 places. He said a heavy snatch workout would have bumped his grade to a B-plus and suggested CrossFit make all Open workouts straightforward AMRAPs for simpler, more accurate scoring.

Making Amends

Hiller Apologizes to Taylor Self — Live on CF Network

Andrew Hiller joined Sevan and Taylor Self live on the CF Network YouTube channel for a roughly 30-minute conversation. Hiller opened by apologizing to Taylor. He said it "rips at his brain" having friends in the space while also wanting to make content, and that he felt like a hypocrite for not making certain videos because of his loyalty to the group. He originally planned to wait until Wodapalooza to talk in person but decided not to let it go on any longer. Sevan said the trust in the original group was "crazy" and that when three people (JY, Tyler, and Hiller) left, it "rocked" them. Taylor said he doesn't hate Hiller and the conversation appeared genuine.
