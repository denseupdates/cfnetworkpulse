## Headline
PushPress CEO Calls Out Berkshire Partners in Open Letter — "They Are Killing CrossFit"

## Article Body (under 300 words — self-contained, facts only)

PushPress CEO Dan Uyemura published an open letter to Berkshire Partners this week accusing the private equity firm of destroying CrossFit from the inside. The letter, which quickly circulated across CrossFit media and was discussed live on Souza's Show on the CF Network, lays out a detailed timeline of what Uyemura calls a botched ownership transition.

Uyemura argues that CrossFit's entire value is its community and affiliate network, not its trademarks or media rights. He says Berkshire's public announcement of the sale 12 months ago — during the 2025 CrossFit Open — triggered a slow crisis of confidence among affiliate owners. He points to a string of failed buyer negotiations, including BeSport, TPG, and most recently Wade Diebner and Mark Mastrov, as evidence that the gap between Berkshire's asking price and what buyers think CrossFit is worth is wide.

The letter defends former CEO Don Faul, who stepped down March 6. Uyemura describes Faul as "a handcuffed man" who genuinely loved CrossFit but was constrained by an ownership group focused on investor returns over community health.

Among the data points cited: 2025 Open registrations dropped 32% (the lowest since 2014), Hyrox has grown to 650,000 competitors and 5,000 affiliated gyms, and affiliate business coach Chris Cooper is now publicly questioning whether the $4,500 affiliation fee is worth it.

Uyemura's proposed solution is straightforward — hire a CEO who puts the community first, then give them actual authority to lead without board interference. He offered his own counsel to Berkshire but made clear he is not seeking the CEO role.

The letter closes with a direct message to Berkshire partner Marni Payne: "The brand isn't dead. The community is extraordinary. But loyalty has a limit, and the clock is running."
